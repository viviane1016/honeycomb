## Guidance

Plans inform everything downstream. Invest in the queen's context: include the queen-file in pre-flight checks, ensure honeycomb coverage for the domain you're planning in, and remember that the scribe inherits the queen's recall trace. If a plan-stage failure leaves you blocked, the debug stage can ask the queen to diagnose and propose actions — it's a tool for mid-pipeline recovery.

Before finalising the work breakdown, scan for files likely to exceed ~500 lines after this feature lands, or any single file more than one builder will modify in the same wave. If you find either, add a wave-0 pre-split spec tagged `[claude-sonnet-4-6]` that lands the structural change first — subsequent feature specs then depend-on the pre-split unit. This keeps parallel builders out of merge-conflict territory and prevents the codebase from drifting toward unreviewable monoliths. See `agentic-file-structure` for the underlying heuristic.
The queen can emit two types of structured updates. When she observes project-specific knowledge during planning — codebase conventions, recurring patterns, past failure modes, or model calibration notes — she may emit a `<<<QUEEN FILE UPDATE>>>...<<<END>>>` block; bees extracts it to `.bees/<slug>/queen-file-proposal.md` for operator review (no auto-apply, one block per plan). When she must improvise around a gap in honeycomb guidance, she may emit a `<<<PALACE PROPOSAL <repo-relative-path>>>>...<<<END>>>` petition; prefer amending an existing room over creating a new one, keep the proposed text terse, and emit at most one petition per gap. The queen file is injected at plan and spec stages only — builder cells do not receive it.
